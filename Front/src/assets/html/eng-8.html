Here are some other musical notations you should be aware of.

The repeat sign indicates a part that should be repeated. You should repeat from the opening repeat sign, or, if there is none, from the beginning. Here is a case without an opening repeat sign.

The octave higher (8va) sign indicates to play this part an octave higher than notated. Similarly, the octave lower (8vb) sign tells us to play an octave lower.

Often times, you will see notes under a curved line. This means to play them legato, or smoothly and connectedly.

On the other hand, staccato, marked with dots on the ends of the notes, tells us to play the note short and apart.
